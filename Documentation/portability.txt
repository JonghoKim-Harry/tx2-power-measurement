For portability with mere measurement overhead, I used:

   * C Standard-compatible Linux System Calls:

        - clock_gettime()              in <time.h>
        - nanosleep()                  in <time.h>
        - exit()                       in <stdlib.h>


   * C Standard Macros and Data Types:

        - assert()                     in <assert.h>
        - int32_t                      in <stdint.h>
        - int64_t                      in <stdint.h>
        - struct timespec              in <time.h>
        - struct tm


   * POSIX-compatible Linux System Calls:

        - gettimeofday()               in <sys/time.h>
        - access()                     in <unistd.h>
        - open()                       in <fcntl.h>
        - lseek()                      in <unistd.h>
        - read()                       in <unistd.h>
        - pread()                      in <unistd.h>
        - write()                      in <unistd.h>
        - dup2()                       in <unistd.h>
        - close()                      in <unistd.h>
        - fork()                       in <unistd.h>
        - execve()                     in <unistd.h>
        - waitpid()                    in <sys/wait.h>
