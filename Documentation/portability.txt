For portability with mere measurement overhead, I used:

   * C Standard-compatible Linux System Calls:

        - clock_gettime()              in <time.h>
        - nanosleep()                  in <time.h>
        - exit()                       in <stdlib.h>

   * POSIX-compatible Linux System Calls:

        - gettimeofday()               in <sys/time.h>
        - stat()                       in <sys/stat.h>
        - mkdir()                      in <sys/stat.h>
        - chown()                      in <unistd.h>
        - geteuid()                    in <unistd.h>
        - access()                     in <unistd.h>
        - open()                       in <fcntl.h>
        - lseek()                      in <unistd.h>
        - read()                       in <unistd.h>
        - pread()                      in <unistd.h>
        - write()                      in <unistd.h>
        - dup2()                       in <unistd.h>
        - close()                      in <unistd.h>
        - fork()                       in <unistd.h>
        - execvp()                     in <unistd.h>
        - waitpid()                    in <sys/wait.h>
